On the maxima of Littlewood polynomials on [−1, 1]

with Mehtaab Sawhney
To appear on arXiv
Paper / notes
Read abstract

A Littlewood polynomial is a polynomial of the form \[ f_n(x)=\sum_{k=0}^n \varepsilon_k x^k \] with $\varepsilon_k\in\{-1, 1\}$. Let $(\varepsilon_k)$ be i.i.d.\ Rademacher coefficients. We show that the lower envelope of $\max_{x\in[-1,1]}|f_n(x)|$ is determined by the small-ball probability of a certain Gaussian process. In particular, almost surely, \[ \liminf_{n\to\infty} \frac{\log(\max_{x\in[-1,1]}|f_n(x)|/\sqrt n)}{(\log\log n)^{1/3}} = -\Big(\frac{3\pi^2}{4}\Big)^{1/3}. \]

A generalization of Grünbaum’s inequality

with Vlad Yaskin
Israel Journal of Mathematics
Accepted
arXiv
Read abstract

Gr\"unbaum's inequality gives sharp bounds between the volume of a convex body and its part cut off by a hyperplane through the centroid of the body. We provide a generalization of this inequality for hyperplanes that do not necessarily contain the centroid. As an application, we obtain a sharp inequality that compares sections of a convex body to the maximal section parallel to it.
